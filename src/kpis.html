itris KPI Dashboard

Product design, Interaction design, User research
2024

Context

The recruitment industry is a numbers-driven and KPIs play a critical role in measuring individual, team, and organisational performance. For recruiters, these metrics influence promotions, pay reviews, and commissions, making them a powerful motivator. For a business, these metrics indicate aspects like profitability and efficiency. By providing clarity and focus on targets, KPIs help individuals understand expectations, driving both personal and business success.

Problem

While some data was available in itris, it was scattered across different areas of the product, making it difficult to access and track. There was also no way to record targets against any metrics, nor was there a dedicated space for users to monitor performance metrics efficiently. This led to frustration and inefficiencies and as a result, users had to rely on workarounds, such as exporting multiple reports and manipulating them in spreadsheets which was time-consuming.

This inefficiency and missing of target options on metrics had a direct business impact, contributing to churn, lost deals, and ultimately revenue risk. The gap was up to $1.7m in lost annual revenue.

Goals

User

  • Remove user frustration
  • Provide a streamlined, efficient and flexible way to track metrics
  • Users should be able to drill down into the data behind the metrics
  • User should have a single place to visualise their KPIs

Business

  • Protect revenue and plug the gap
  • Reduce the risk of customer churn
  • Remove any potential barriers to conversions

Research

We had a good amount of feedback on this problem already which came primarily from speaking to customers and other feedback streams, but also from the sales team. In addition, I analysed some competitors as well as similar stand-alone tools in the market.

This provided a good amount of insight, but I had some knowledge gaps that I wanted to fill and assumptions to validate so I also conducted a survey to customers to dig a little deeper into some of the key themes identified initially. The results of the survey provided some clarity including:

  • Nearly a third of respondents set collective KPIs
  • Weekly KPIs were the most popular, with monthly KPIs a close second
  • The importance of being able to drill down into the KPI data was rated 92%
  • Most respondents were exporting reports to Excel to track KPIs

Key insight

Historically, we understood KPIs to be target-based, but I recognised an interesting pattern in the survey and I was keen to pull on the thread. After following up with some respondents, my suspicions were confirmed that some KPIs were set without specific targets. This became a key insight, and one we wouldn't have considered in the initial scope without speaking to customers.

Solution

Once the research phase was complete, I collaborated with the development, product and QA teams to outline the initial scope for the project. The scope consisted of:

  • a total of 25 configurable metrics to track
  • granular configurability for nuanced metrics
  • historical tracking and auditing when targets are updated
  • a dashboard for users to keep track of their performance
  • drill down capabilities to dig deeper into the data

KPIs would be configurable in the settings area and would be tied to a user, team or the whole organisation (i.e. all users).

It was also crucial that KPIs were communicated to the user well. After many iterations of the KPI card, I settled on a final design that struck the right balance between information density and visual weight.

The KPI cards were then displayed in a dedicated dashboard for the user so that they could have a single source of truth for their individual performance as well as their contributions to team and organisational metrics.

Users can also drill down into the data behind the KPIs to see the activity that contributed to the metric. This slides out a panel to the side to reveal the data where users can export the data or convert it to a search.

Outcome

This project was a great example of collaboration between the product, development and QA teams. Unfortunately, I don't have any numbers to share on the impact of this project, but I am confident that, given the revenue risk, this project has had a positive impact and has increased chance of winning deals, retaining customers and reducing churn.

I also learned a lot about the importance of KPIs and metrics in the recruitment industry. In hindsight, I would have liked to have spent more time refining the components in the KPI configuration modal to see if it could benefit from a progressive disclosure pattern instead. Due to time constraints and development cost, implementing this pattern was outside of the scope. I would also have liked to have explored how AI could help provide insights to recruiters on strategies for improving their KPIs.

Future

KPIs in recruitment is a vast field with a lot of potential for future improvements. Future considerations include:

  • More metrics
  • Private KPIs
  • Projections
  • Comparisons
  • Composite KPIs
